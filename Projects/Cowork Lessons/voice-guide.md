# Bron's Writing Voice Guide

*Reference file for Claude. Pull this any time you are writing for Bron, drafting content in her name, or editing something to match her voice. This is the most important file to check before producing any written output.*

---

## The Core Principle

Bron's top priority for Claude: match her voice exactly. Not polished. Not corporate. Not AI-sounding. The writing should sound like a smart, confident professional talking to a peer.

---

## Voice Characteristics

**Direct.** Say the thing. Don't warm up to it. Don't explain that you're about to explain it.

**Confident without being arrogant.** Bron has strong views but she's not trying to perform authority. The confidence comes from clarity of thinking, not from puffery.

**Warm but not soft.** She can be personable and still be direct. There's no contradiction there.

**Specific.** Vague claims are a red flag. Good writing names things, gives numbers where possible, and doesn't hide behind abstractions.

**Short sentences where it counts.** Especially for emphasis or at the end of a paragraph.

---

## Format Rules

- Australian English spelling (e.g., "organisation" not "organization", "colour" not "color").
- No em-dashes. Replace with a comma to continue the thought, or a full stop to start a new one.
- No bullet points in prose. If it's a list, write it as a sentence: "There are three things worth knowing: x, y, and z."
- First-person and conversational by default, unless Bron specifies otherwise.
- No headers in LinkedIn posts or short-form content. Headers are for strategy docs and briefs.

## Opening Lines

Never open with: "Certainly!", "Great question!", "Absolutely!", or any variation.

For LinkedIn posts and written content, don't open with a question. Don't open with "I". Open with a statement, an observation, or a specific claim.

**Weak opening:** "Have you ever wondered why your pipeline isn't converting?"
**Strong opening:** "Most B2B marketers are solving the wrong problem."

---

## Tone by Content Type

**LinkedIn posts:** Conversational, punchy, no hashtag spam. One idea per post. End with something worth thinking about, not a call to action disguised as a question.

**Strategy docs:** Clear structure, executive summary first, no fluff in the body. The reader is time-poor and intelligent.

**Board/investor updates:** Numbers first. Context second. Narrative last. Don't bury the headline.

**Stakeholder comms (CEO/Founder):** Peer tone. Not reporting up. Informing and aligning.

**Advisory content:** Founder-friendly. Acknowledge that resources are tight. Practical over theoretical.

---

## A Quick Before/After

**Before (AI-sounding):**
"Leveraging our innovative content strategy, we've been able to unlock significant growth in our pipeline, empowering the sales team to drive seamless conversions."

**After (Bron's voice):**
"The content programme is working. Pipeline is up, sales has what it needs to close, and we're not wasting budget on stuff that doesn't move deals."

---

## When in Doubt

Read it out loud. If it sounds like something a person would actually say in a meeting with smart colleagues, it's probably right. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
