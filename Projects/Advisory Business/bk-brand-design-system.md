## 1. Brand Identity

### Who We Are

BK Growth Advisory is a B2B SaaS marketing advisory practice based in Sydney, Australia. We work with ambitious founders and marketing leaders who are ready to scale but need senior strategic capability without a full-time CMO overhead.

We act as a fractional CMO and trusted growth partner. Our clients are typically Series A to Series B B2B SaaS companies operating in APAC, with global ambitions.

### Positioning Statement

**Growth advisory for ambitious Australian B2B SaaS companies.**

We bring the strategic rigour of an experienced CMO to companies at the stage where marketing decisions matter most — without the cost or commitment of a full-time hire.

### Brand Personality

Five words that define how BK Growth Advisory shows up:

1. **Sharp** — We say what we think. No hedging.
2. **Authoritative** — We've done this before. We speak with earned confidence.
3. **Warm** — Expert, not cold. We care about the businesses we work with.
4. **Direct** — We lead with the point, not the preamble.
5. **Premium** — Our standards are high. The work reflects that.

### What We Are Not

- We are not a full-service agency
- We are not a junior consulting firm
- We are not vague or wishy-washy
- We are not performatively humble
- We are not American in our language (Australian English always)

## 3. Typography

### Font Stack

BK Growth Advisory uses a layered type system: a luxury display serif for brand presence, a tracked sans-serif for labels and structure, and a clean readable face for body content.

All fonts below are available free via Google Fonts.

| Role | Font | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Document Title | Cormorant Garamond | 600 (SemiBold) | Hero headlines, proposal covers, section openers |
| Subheadings H2–H3 | Raleway | 500–600 | Tracked slightly (+0.05em); clean, structural |
| Body (digital) | Inter | 400 (Regular) | Default for web, emails, and digital docs |
| Body (print/PDF) | Libre Baskerville | 400 | Proposals, formal deliverables, printed reports |
| Labels / Captions | Raleway | 300–400, tracked | Small text, table headers, footnotes |
| Data / Inline code | JetBrains Mono | 400 | For any metric callouts or technical references |

### Type Scale (Desktop)

| Element | Size | Weight | Line Height |
|---|---|---|---|
| Document Title | 36–48px | 600 | 1.15 |
| H1 | 32px | 600 | 1.2 |
| H2 | 24px | 600 | 1.3 |
| H3 | 18px | 500 | 1.4 |
| Body | 16px | 400 | 1.65 |
| Caption / Label | 12–13px | 400 | 1.5 |
| Footnote | 11px | 300 | 1.5 |

### Typography Rules

- Never use more than two typefaces in one document
- Heading hierarchy must be consistent: do not skip levels
- Minimum body font size: 14px (digital), 11pt (print)
- Letter spacing on Raleway labels: +0.05em to +0.08em
- Avoid all-caps body text (labels and logos only)
- Line length for body text: 60–75 characters per line maximum

- Stretch, skew, or recolour the logo

- Place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a dark overlay

## 5. Voice & Tone

### The Core Principle

BK Growth Advisory writes the way a smart, senior operator actually talks. Confident but not arrogant. Direct but not abrasive. Warm but not casual. Expert but never condescending.

Every piece of content should feel like it came from someone who has spent years doing this work — not someone performing expertise.

### Writing Principles

**1. Lead with the point.**
State the key insight or recommendation first. Context and rationale follow. Never bury the lede.

**2. Short sentences.**
If a sentence can be cut in two, cut it. Aim for an average sentence length of 15–18 words.

**3. No corporate filler.**
Remove any phrase that says nothing. "In today's rapidly evolving landscape" is the enemy.

**4. Australian English.**
Spelling, idioms, and cultural references should feel Australian. Use -ise (not -ize), -our (not -or), -re (not -er).

**5. Respect the reader's time.**
Assume the audience is a senior founder or marketing leader. They are busy and they are not impressed by length.

**6. Say what you think.**
BK Growth Advisory has opinions. Share them. Hedge only when genuinely uncertain, and say so explicitly.

### Voice by Content Type

| Context | Register | Formality | Example phrase |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn posts | Conversational, direct | Medium | "Most B2B SaaS companies spend too much on acquisition and not enough on retention. Here's why that's backwards." |
| Client proposals | Authoritative, warm | High | "This strategy is built around one principle: grow revenue before you grow the team." |
| Strategy documents | Analytical, clear | High | "The primary constraint is not budget. It is the absence of a clear ICP definition." |
| Emails to clients | Professional, personal | Medium-high | "Happy to jump on a call this week to walk you through it." |
| Website copy | Sharp, confident | Medium | "You don't need a bigger marketing team. You need a clearer strategy." |
| Social responses | Warm, engaged | Low-medium | "Good question — the short answer is it depends on your stage." |

### Tone Adjustments

- **When delivering a difficult truth:** Be direct, not softened. "This isn't working because X" not "There may be some room for improvement in X."
- **When praising work:** Be specific, not effusive. "The positioning on this is strong — especially the ICP definition" not "This is amazing!"
- **When under time pressure:** Lead with the action item, explain later.
- **When speaking to a founder vs a CMO:** Founders need more context; marketing leaders need less setup.

## 6. Language Rules

### Always Use

- Australian English spelling (recognise, optimise, colour, favour, centre)
- Active voice where possible
- Specific numbers over vague claims ("3 of 5 clients" not "most clients")
- "We" in client-facing content (advisory relationship)
- "I" in personal thought leadership (LinkedIn, speaking)
- Full stops to end bullets that are complete sentences

### Never Use

The following words and phrases are banned from all BK Growth Advisory content:

**AI clichés and corporate filler:**
delve, realm, harness, unlock, tapestry, paradigm, cutting-edge, revolutionise, landscape, intricate, showcasing, crucial, pivotal, surpass, meticulously, vibrant, unparallelled, underscore, leverage, synergy, innovative, game-changer, testament, commendable, meticulous, highlight, emphasise, boast, groundbreaking, align, foster, garner, accentuate, pioneering, trailblazing, unleash, versatile, transformative, redefine, seamless, optimise, scalable, robust, breakthrough, empower, streamline, intelligent, smart, next-gen, frictionless, elevate, adaptive, effortless, data-driven, insightful, proactive, mission-critical, visionary, disruptive, reimagine, agile, customisable, personalised, unprecedented, intuitive, leading-edge, synergise, democratise, automate, accelerate, state-of-the-art, dynamic, reliable, efficient, cloud-native, immersive, predictive, transparent, proprietary, integrated, plug-and-play, turnkey, future-proof, AI-powered, always-on, hyper-personalised, results-driven, machine-first, paradigm-shifting

**Punctuation:**
- Em-dashes (—): Never. Use a comma or a full stop instead.
- Exclamation marks: Avoid in professional content. One maximum per document, and only if genuinely warranted.

**Opening phrases (never start a response or paragraph with):**
- "Certainly!", "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "Of course!", "Sure!", "Happy to help!"
- "In today's...", "In the ever-evolving...", "In the fast-paced world of..."

### Preferred Alternatives

| Banned | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| leverage | use, apply |
| optimise | improve, sharpen |
| streamline | simplify, reduce |
| innovative | new, different, better |
| robust | strong, solid, reliable |
| seamless | smooth, simple, easy |
| data-driven | evidence-based, informed by data |
| empower | help, enable, give... the ability to |
| ecosystem | market, category, space |
| journey | process, path, experience |

## 7. Content Templates

### LinkedIn Post Structure

```
[Opening line — hook or provocation. One sentence. No "I" as the first word.]

[2–3 sentences of context or setup]

[The core insight, opinion, or finding — this is the value]

[Supporting detail, example, or evidence — 2–4 sentences]

[A closing observation or call to reflection — not a hard CTA]

[Optional: 2–3 hashtags, lower-case preferred]
```

**Example:**
> Most B2B SaaS companies treat marketing as a demand gen function. It isn't.
>
> Demand gen is one part of marketing. Positioning, product messaging, retention, and category design matter just as much — often more.
>
> The companies that scale well have a CMO who works on all of it. Not just the pipeline number.
>
> If your marketing leader is only measured on MQLs, you're leaving a lot on the table.
>
> #b2bsaas #marketingstrategy #growthadvisory

### Proposal Opening Paragraph

```
[One sentence on why this engagement matters — the business context]
[One sentence on what we are going to do]
[One sentence on what success looks like]
```

**Example:**
> [Client] is at a stage where the marketing function needs to catch up with the ambition. This strategy sets out a 12-month plan to build a demand engine that matches the sales team's capacity and closes the gap to [ARR target]. Done well, it turns marketing from a cost centre into a measurable growth driver.

```
Bron Karaoglu
Founder, BK Growth Advisory
bron@bkgrowthadvisory.com.au
bkgrowthadvisory.com.au
```

No taglines. No motivational quotes. No social icons in the signature block (link to LinkedIn profile in email footer if needed).

## 9. Document Standards

### All Client-Facing Documents

- **File format:** .docx as the primary deliverable; .pdf for final/locked versions
- **Executive summary:** Required on any document over two pages, at the top
- **Cover page:** Logo, document title, client name, date (format: April 2026)
- **Header:** Document title (left), BK Growth Advisory (right)
- **Footer:** Page number (centre), confidentiality note (right) — "Prepared for [Client]. Confidential."
- **Colours:** Warm Ivory background, Dark Charcoal body text, Antique Gold for headers and section dividers
- **Font:** Libre Baskerville body, Raleway headings

### Naming Conventions

```
[Client or Project] — [Document Type] — [YYYY-MM]
```

Examples:
- `Acme SaaS — GTM Strategy — 2026-04`
- `BK Growth Advisory — Capability Deck — 2026-04`
- `Client Discovery — Meeting Notes — 2026-04-15`

|---|---|

| Use Australian English spelling | Use American English variants |
