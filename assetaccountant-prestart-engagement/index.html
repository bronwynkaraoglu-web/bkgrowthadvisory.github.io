05 · Where this fits

Three ways to use the six weeks before Day One.

You could spend the pre-start window in three different ways. Worth naming them honestly.

01
Do nothing

Wait for 27 July. Start cold.

Perfectly legitimate. Every CMO eventually arrives without a head start. The cost is that the first 90 days are spent on intelligence work that could have been done earlier, slowing the move to execution.

My honest verdict: workable, but it pushes real growth work from Q3 into Q4 and beyond.
02
Onboarding light

I read the materials and meet the team informally.

Useful, but it does not produce anything that changes the year-one plan. It is the version of onboarding most companies run by default. Calendar fills with intros, no commercial output.

My honest verdict: comfortable. Probably the lowest return on the calendar window.

10 · How I work

How I work

Six principles. Common sense on paper. Easy to drift from in practice. These hold for the sprint and for the CMO seat that follows.

  1. Strategy before execution

    Speed matters. Speed without direction burns budget. The first task is always to define who to pursue and why, clearly enough that everyone can act on it from day one.

  2. ICP before investment

    Most pipeline problems are targeting problems. We define exactly who AssetAccountant wins with, and why, before adding budget, headcount, or campaigns.

  3. Fix the story before scaling spend

    More budget behind a weak message creates more noise. The narrative gets resolved first. Then we build the engine to deliver it.

  4. Own pipeline outcomes, not activity

    Marketing is accountable for pipeline generation, CAC payback, and NRR contribution. Not content volume. Not impressions.

  5. Build systems that compound

    A campaign ends. A well-built GTM engine compounds. The goal is to leave something that keeps working, not to be needed indefinitely.

  6. Bring the founders along, do not work around them

    The best GTM moves come from founders and marketing in the same room. I work with you on the strategic calls, not around you.
