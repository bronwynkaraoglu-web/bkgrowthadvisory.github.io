What I heard

  • The fundamental problem in North America is predictable outbound pipeline. The current motion is too broad across a large database with limited signal on who the right target actually is. Outbound effectiveness sits at 2.8% win rate against an inbound rate of 25.6%. The product is not the issue. The targeting is.
  • Of roughly 15,000 CRM records, only around 200 have an accounting stack assigned. That gap is the core operational problem this engagement needs to close. The accounting stack is a strong leading indicator, not because QuickBooks or Xero is the target, but because using accounting software signals a reconciliation pain Re-Leased can solve. The task is finding other observable signals that identify those same buyers before they raise their hand, because there is almost zero digital search traffic in commercial real estate. "I would not like to come out of this body of work in the same situation where we have 15,000 records and only 200 people with QuickBooks assigned." — Dai
  • The tactical work is already moving. The CRE fit score is being refined by Dulan off the back of the win/loss analysis. Closed-lost re-engagement is in motion. What is missing is the strategic layer: a clear, agreed picture of the primary targets, the signals that identify them, and the segment-specific messaging that makes the outbound motion coherent across the whole team.
  • First-touch conversion in the US sits at around 11%. When those same prospects re-enter the funnel later, conversion jumps to 30% and above. That tells you two things. First, the nurture motion matters enormously, the right customers just need time and consistent air cover. Second, getting the primary targets right upfront determines who is worth nurturing over the long run. Credia AI is being attached to 100% of deals and will become a named third package in July. That expands the revenue story once the right customers are in the door.
  • Some segments get a hard no. The data is clear: certain customer types win at 56%, others at 0%. Part of the value of this engagement is a documented negative target list so the whole outbound team knows where not to spend time. For the segments that sit in the middle, the output includes how to treat them differently, a lighter-touch motion or inbound only, rather than applying the same outbound intensity across everything.
  • Canada is a distinct market, not a US extension. Sales activity on the ground in Canada is increasing from mid-year. The sprint covers both, treated separately.

The opportunity

Your UK business averages NZD $27,000 ARR per customer. North America averages NZD $9,000. That is not a product gap. It is a focus gap. The UK has a clearer picture of who it sells to and how. North America is still testing. Companies that define their primary targets before scaling their outbound motion consistently see win rates improve by 15 to 25 percentage points within two quarters, because every part of the motion, targeting, message, qualification, and follow-up, is built for the same buyer. The path from $9K to $27K average ARR in North America runs through knowing exactly who you are going after and giving your whole team the tools to find them, reach them, and win them. That is what this sprint is designed to do.

How I work

  1. Strategy before execution. Speed matters. But speed without direction burns dollars. The first task is to define who to pursue and why, clearly enough that the whole outbound team can act on it from day one.
  2. Primary targets before outbound investment. Most outbound problems are targeting problems. In a market with almost zero digital search traffic, where you have to know who you are going after before you reach out, getting this right is not optional.
  3. Signals, not just tech stack. The accounting stack is a leading indicator, not a destination. The real work is identifying other observable signals, from job postings, website structure, portfolio type, company stage, that tell you a prospect is a primary target before you call them. The qualitative work is where this comes from.
  4. Hard nos matter as much as primary targets. A documented negative target list is part of the output. The data is clear that some segments are not worth the outbound investment. Saying so clearly saves time, budget, and morale.
  5. Frameworks that stay in the business. A campaign ends. A segment model, a messaging hierarchy, and a qualification framework keep paying. Jana will be on all customer interview calls so the capability transfers, not just the document.
  6. Measure outbound effectiveness, not average ARR. Pipeline ARR by segment and outbound effectiveness by segment are the agreed measures. Average ARR carries too much discount and timing noise. The measures are agreed upfront and the sprint is accountable to them.
